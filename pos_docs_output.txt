========== 25- account advice.docx ==========
Technical Flow - Accounting System for POS

1?? Create a Dedicated Accounting Module
Start by planning and structuring an Accounting Module in your backend. This module will be responsible for tracking all financial activity (sales, purchases, payments, returns, expenses) using standard accounting principles.

2?? Define a Chart of Accounts (COA)
Set up a tree-like structure of account categories:
Assets (Cash, Bank, Inventory, Accounts Receivable)
Liabilities (Accounts Payable, Loans)
Revenue (Sales, Service Income)
Expenses (Purchases, Rent, Salaries, Utilities)
Equity (Capital, Retained Earnings)
Each financial action will be recorded against these accounts.

3?? Set Up Ledger Accounts
Each account in the Chart of Accounts will have a Ledger Account that holds the real-time balance and transaction history. Every sale, purchase, payment, or return will affect one or more ledger accounts.

4?? Design Journal Entries and Double-Entry System
All financial events must be logged using a double-entry accounting system, where:
One account is debited
Another account is credited
The total amount of debits always equals the total amount of credits
Example:
A sale of 10,000 CFA to a customerDebit: Accounts ReceivableCredit: Sales Revenue

5?? Implement Accounting Lifecycle Rules
Each transaction should follow a secure lifecycle:
Draft: Editable state
Locked: No more edits (awaiting approval)
Verified: Final confirmation (by authorized personnel)
Secured: Cannot be modified anymore
These rules ensure accountability and auditability.

6?? Integrate with Other Modules
Connect the accounting system to all other modules:
POS Module
Trigger
Accounting Effect

Sales Invoice
Confirm sale
Debit client account, credit sales income

Purchase
Receive invoice
Debit inventory/expense, credit supplier account

Client Payment
Receive payment
Debit cash/bank, credit client account

Supplier Payment
Pay supplier
Debit supplier account, credit cash/bank

Refund
Process refund
Reverse the original journal entries

Inventory
Stock adjustment
Record gain/loss as expense or income

Expense
Log payment (e.g. rent, salary)
Debit expense, credit cash/bank


7?? Create a Financial Year System
Define financial years and months. Only allow transactions in open periods. Lock months and years when closed to prevent backdated edits.

8?? Support Opening Balances
When starting a new year or migrating from another system, allow entry of opening balances:
How much cash in bank
How much clients owe
How much you owe suppliers
Value of existing stock
Previous equity or capital

9?? Build Financial Reports
Provide the following reports for finance and business decisions:
Report
Purpose

Trial Balance
Verify that debits equal credits

General Ledger
Show transactions per account

Income Statement
Show profit or loss over time

Balance Sheet
Show financial position (Assets = Liabilities + Equity)

Cash Flow Statement
Show money in/out from operations, investing, etc.


?? Control Access with Roles and Permissions
Define what actions each user role can perform:
Cashiers: can view but not create entries
Accountants: can create and lock entries
Managers: can verify entries
Admins: full control, including secure lock and undo
Use role-based access to protect financial data.

?? Track Every Financial Action
Ensure that:
Every sale, purchase, and payment is automatically posted to the ledger
Every edit is logged
Nothing can be deleted once locked
Users know who created, locked, or verified each transaction

? Final Notes
With this accounting system:
You'll always know where your money is
You'll avoid fraud and error through locking and verification
You'll be ready for audits and financial statements
You'll have professional, ERP-level bookkeeping
